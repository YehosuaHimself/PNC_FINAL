Origin
Where it
grows.

Coffee originates from Ethiopia. Not symbolically — botanically. Every variety cultivated on every farm on Earth descends from wild Coffea arabica first found in the forests of this country. Kochere is a woreda in the Gedeo zone of Yirgacheffe — the highest-altitude coffee-growing area in Ethiopia, at 2,400 metres. At that altitude, the slow ripening season produces a density of flavour that no lower farm can replicate. The jasmine and bergamot notes are not added. They are grown. You are drinking coffee from the place coffee comes from, at the altitude it grows best, for €1 a cup. There is nothing comparable.

Craft · YCFCU
How it is
made.

At the YCFCU cooperative, cherries are pulped within hours of picking. Fermented 36–48 hours in clean water tanks. Dried on raised beds for 10–15 days in direct Yirgacheffe sun — not on the ground, not in shade. From there: roasted in small batches in the Alps, light, taken to first crack at 196°C, stopped there. Rested 48 hours. Ground to medium for immersion. Sealed in unbleached kraft, nitrogen-flushed. Shipped within 7 days of roast. We name every step. Anyone who doesn't is hiding something.

02 · THE TEMPERATURE
92–94°C.
Not boiling.

Boiling water is 100°C. The jasmine and bergamot aromatics in Kochere G1 are heat-volatile above 94°C — they scorch and disappear. Letting the kettle rest 45 seconds after boiling is not a preference. It is the instruction.

03 · THE BAG
Kraft. Unbleached.
Nitrogen-flushed.

Unbleached kraft cellulose — no chlorine, no dioxin bleach. Nitrogen-flushed at packing: the enemy of roasted coffee is oxidation, not time. Sealed: 90 days peak flavour. Composts in 8–12 weeks.

04 · THE GRADE
85+ SCA.
No exceptions.

Kochere G1 scores 85+ on the SCA scale — specialty grade, the top tier. Grind weight, dose, and distribution calibrated at source. You skip the technique. You get the result.

No warehouse. No sitting stock. Roasted the day you order, packed the same afternoon, shipped within 7 days. Specialty coffee at peak freshness — for €1 a cup, anywhere in the world.

The Region
Where it
comes from.

Kochere is a woreda in the Gedeo zone of the Yirgacheffe region — one of the highest-altitude coffee-growing areas on Earth. At 2,100 metres, the slow ripening season produces a density of flavour that no lower-altitude bean can replicate. The jasmine and bergamot notes are not added. They are grown.

The Process
How it is
made.

Sourced directly through AMG Coffee Export, Ethiopia. Fully washed. Dried on raised beds. Traceable to the cooperative. EUDR-compliant. Roasted in small batches in the Alp Mountains — light roast, taken to first crack. Rested 48 hours. Ground to medium, sealed in kraft paper, nitrogen-flushed. Shipped within 7 days of roast date.

— How to Brew

Three
steps.

01
Drop it in.

Place the pad into your cup. The pad is self-contained — no filter, no equipment, nothing else required.

Cup Size
200–250 ml ideal
02
Add hot water.

Pour around 92–94°C directly over the pad. The bloom begins immediately — jasmine first.

Water Temp
92–94°C
03
Watch it bloom.

Leave the pad in. The flavour evolves as it steeps — floral first, then fruity, then a clean dark chocolate finish. Remove when it reaches your preferred strength.

— Why This Format

The design
of zero waste.

01
Zero extraction variables.

The grind, the dose, the distribution — all fixed at source. Every cup is identical. There is no technique to learn and nothing to calibrate.

02
Immersion unlocks this bean.

Kochere G1's complex aromatics are best extracted through full immersion. The pad format is not a compromise — it is the optimal method for this specific coffee.

03
No astringency. By design.

The kraft paper acts as a barrier against over-extraction. The result is a clean, bright cup without bitterness, regardless of how long the pad remains in the water.

04
Farm to cup in 7 days.

Roasted to order. Packed the same day. Shipped within 7 days of roast date. The gap between harvest and your cup is the shortest possible for a product of this quality.
